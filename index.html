OCT - 11

My arm was twitching terribly again, so I decided to give up napping any longer. Above my roof, the sky was covered by its own roof of clouds, darkening the drab neighborhood. The house I live in must look like a piece of Ikea furniture in a thrift shop - the square building was recently repainted and lined with crisply trimmed little bushes, while the rest of the street only has scraggly trees and facades that are peeling. I doubt many people who walk by, if any, know about the squiggly earthquake-signed crack, as it is well hidden behind a perfectly-sized shrub. I’d noticed it when the gardener had forgotten to trim a stray branch, and I completed the job with a pair of scissors. For some reason, I now check behind the bush every time I’m back from another day at EARTHDATA. Sometimes it looks slightly different than the previous day, like a signature re-written with the same hand.

I held my phone to my face and my eyes watered from the screen’s brightness. It was already near to night time; I’d replied to Data Requests in the morning and slept on the sofa through the afternoon. I think Daniel and I texted until midnight yesterday. Sometimes all his stories mix up in my head, as we tend to have lengthy phone calls, but I am pretty sure his opening line went like:

Well, I had breakfast with Dallas Raines and tested my new EARTHNEWS equipment at Signal Hill

I remember enjoying the rest of our conversation, as he described the adventure poetically, without his usual dramatics. Apparently, his Sunday began in an IHOP with Mr. Raines, who accumulated a modest amount of poparazzi, waving fans off with a wide smile, then transitioned to the top of a grassy hill, where he set up some metal device, and watched the city darken and disappear into dots of street lights.

This time however he was more plain:

could I stop by tonight?

I returned with: sure.

For my extremely late lunch, I heated up a bowl of soup I’d made the day before and poured some crackers in. I didn’t ask Daniel why he wanted to visit. My apartment accepted visitors whenever; Penny would come over randomly as well so I wasn’t disturbed. I reasoned, perhaps he wanted to explain his extraordinarily eventful week in person. Finishing the soup, I nibbled at the crackers, occasionally freeing my eyes to the window. There was a tiny patch of sky I hadn’t noticed before, revealing the true, deep navy color of the sky. It was bound to be overtaken by its neighbors.

That instant my phone began buzzing. Daniel was at my doorstep.

“Hey E! The overcast is unusually dense today, perhaps there are cumulonimbus behind these stratocumulus. What have you been doing this dim day?” He gave me a light hug.

“Mhm. I’ve been napping.” I went to the kitchen to prepare tea and spoke to him across the island counter. “What’s the weekend report?”

He came around the counter to lean on the fridge. “HQ says the reconfigured Earth-Core equipment will be ready to test in a couple days, so I’ll be back at Signal for the week. Should be a tidal wave of Data when it comes to collecting, as the last time they drilled at Signal Hill was in the 1960’s, during the oil surplus. So I’ll be within radius to visit you more frequently, if you’ll allow me to reduce your tea supply. But I..” He paused as I poured the boiling water, “Well, I wanted to ask you a question.”

“Yeah?” Unusual, but I dismissed it as he had dramatically asked me to recommend a book of poetry to him once. I lent him my antique copy of a Borzutzky collection.

“Well, have you ever considered how the mechanical systems of earth, such as the hydrologic cycle, would simply run, yet sustained us all?”

Technically, this was a question, but it was not the question. I often didn’t think of historical weather systems anymore, since it’s nearly all regulated now. Also, I know many natural disasters used to devastate whole islands, so I don’t have as pure a view of cloud formation as Daniel does. I shook my head.

Daniel has slightly above average length legs that remind me of an insect when he sits cross-legged. Today though, we sat on the couch. We hesitantly sipped the steaming tea, and I could’ve sworn I noticed slight worry holding up his eyebrows.

“I’ve been thinking about the parts that sustain my own systems. You’ve definitely taken a vital part, perhaps to the extent of the ocean.”

I could’ve guessed he relied upon me so much. After all, I’m the only other friend besides Penny that he visits randomly.

“You’re, um, a constant to me, and I wanted to thank you.”

“Oh, you’re welcome.” I wondered whether that was all, but his expression made me uneasy. His brows kept skewing, quivering slightly. Then he looked away and sighed.

I built up a question myself, while double checking that my own forehead was relaxed, free of the slight crease that sometimes appeared above my brow. “Are you worried about something?”

His brows raised further upwards, then he straightened his face out and looked serious.

He finally asked, “Well, E, would you want to go out with me?”

My stomach felt like it was the ocean, only being forced into a small sphere. Not only is my newest job occupying 6 days of the week, but he’s a friend to me to the same degree as Penny and sometimes I doubt we’ve ever had a conversation. All I know is that he is certain and I am not.

I told him I’d think about it.

He left with a side hug and a grin. Penny texted me later, and as soon as I told her he’d visited, she surprised me:

Oh, did he come over to at last reveal his friendship had turned romantic??

She knew he was going to ask, possibly encouraged him. I wonder if she knows how a stomach can feel like a contained ocean.

Getting sleepy now, signing off.

I knew this place was a feat of architecture, about the size of a small town in which you never need to go outside, since even the station is connected through a concrete tunnel (which is probably destroyed now). Yet I cannot exit the building, or find the exit. I did find that Datanews HQ is the next division over within this structure, so I found myself wandering down the hallways which were unblocked by debris. I haven’t been interrupted by any other curious victims; it seems everyone cleared out at their shift’s end. As I made my way down the half-lit corridors, it hit me that this was my chance to resolve my suspicions.

The interiors remind me of an outdated airport, although without any stores. Instead, there is an occasional door, windowless and labeled with dark green numbers. All were locked, and made a dense metal sound when I tapped on them. At last I came to a door which was unhinged and leaning off the frame. It was not numbered, but instead had ‘EARTHNEWS SR Room’ written on the label. I do RS, so SR would mean System-to-Reality. Which is odd, and could be the evidence I’m searching for. Now I am staring the equipment inside. It looks identical to my own - the Immersion Box is attached with metal rods to the ceiling, hovering near the System Desk. The only differences are the label, reading ENSR, and the chair, being deep green and in better condition than my own. It is still on - a dot of light on the box flashes in saturated red.

Daniel or Penny might try to believe me, for the sake of empathy, but they love their jobs. They’d never be able to risk it. I’m going to enter the DNSR - wish me luck.
